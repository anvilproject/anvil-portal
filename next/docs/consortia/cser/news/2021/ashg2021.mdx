# CSER at ASHG 2021

<Subheader>18 October, 2021</Subheader>

CSER investigators are leading a number of posters and presentations that are featured in the upcoming <Link label="American Society of Human Genetics Virtual Meeting 2021" url="https://www.ashg.org/meetings/2021meeting/" />, which is held virtually and scheduled to run from October 18-22, 2021.

A full list of CSER work at the ASHG 2021 virtual meeting <Link label="can be found online here in Google Doc format" url="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Htv1O3uKx0Pi5YKyn7I-VPqL22KoHw3ImsFXiyl7-cU/edit?usp=sharing" /> for easy reference, and are included below:

### Poster Sessions

**October 18, 2021 (Poster Sessions)**

- **Henoke Shiferaw (ClinSeq)**. <Link label="Poster PrgmNr 3116" url="https://eventpilotadmin.com/web/page.php?page=Session&amp;project=ASHG21&amp;id=P2522" />.\
Identification of polyadenylation signals relevant to Mendelian disease variant interpretation. <NBS>(1:30 - 2:30pm ET)</NBS>
- **Sharon Plon (KidsCanSeq)**. <Link label="Poster PrgmNr 3220" url="https://eventpilotadmin.com/web/page.php?page=Session&amp;project=ASHG21&amp;id=P2626" />.\
Does it make sense to report single pathogenic alleles in cancer susceptibility genes for childhood
cancer patients? Results from the BASIC3 and Texas KidsCanSeq studies. <NBS>(3:30 - 4:30pm ET)</NBS>
- **Anne Slavotinek (P3EGS)**. <Link label="Poster PrgmNr 3800" url="https://eventpilotadmin.com/web/page.php?page=Session&amp;project=ASHG21&amp;id=P3206" />.\
